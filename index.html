Internet access in New York City is an affordability issue

By Alexandra White

Nov. 10 2020

The pandemic has made New York City’s plan to expand affordable broadband connection in all five boroughs essential. According to data from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey, 14.7% of New York City households were without internet in 2019. In response, Mayor Bill de Blasio created an internet master plan in 2019 that would invest $157 million in closing the digital divide.

Most families who do not have internet access cannot afford internet. “This is an affordability issue at its core,” said John Paul Farmer, chief technology officer for the city of New York, at a virtual hearing hosted by the City Council to discuss the digital divide. “My neighbor down the block not being able to afford broadband becomes a public health issue,” said Farmer.

According to census data from 2016, households with the lowest household income have the highest percentage of no internet access. As expected, households with the highest household income have the lowest percentage of no internet access. Mayor Bill de Blasio plans on working with smaller internet service providers in New York City to increase competition, which could lead to more affordable broadband internet plans.
